// About Me

Hey! Thanks for checking me out :)

I just recently received my BS in Computer Science from Northern Arizona University, and am actively pursuing software engineering jobs in several fields. Out of all areas that I've studied, full-stack web development is what I'm most experienced with, but I've also had professional exposure to IoT and low-level OS programming while working at several NAU labs. I would love to dive deeper into full-stack development, but I also wouldn't mind learning and working in a different programmatic context. Ideally, I would be working for a company who is either exposed to the outdoor industry (think Garmin, OnX, Strava, etc.), or similary involved in conserving or improving the natural state of our precious blue dot.

You probably learned about my professional experience in my cover letter and resume, so I'll fill in some holes about the rest of my life with a wordy bulleted list:

  • I love being in nature, and getting outdoors for extended periods of time will always be a priority of mine for many reasons.

  • I'm totally hooked on climbing right now, especially adventurous multi-pitch and big-wall climbing. It's such an intense way to interact with nature, and the partnerships I've made with others through this activity have proven to be deep and everlasting.

  • I've lived in Flagstaff, AZ for nearly 4 years, and would love to stay a little longer if I can make it work professionally.

  • I moved from California to Wisconsin when I was 9, and lived just north of Madison Until I was 19. Before you ask, the answer is no, I'm not much of a cheesehead.

  • I have one older sister who is a mega-badass environmental policy analyst. She went to George Washington University in D.C. for her undergrad, and just recently received her MPA from UW Seattle.

  • I'm a huge animal lover. For this and other environmental reasons, I subsist on a mostly plant-based diet.

  • While I haven't run much recently, I do like to go the distance. I ran a 50 mile ultramarathon in 2020, and my stoke to run long has finally returned after pacing my partner through her first 100-miler in June.

  • My partner! Her name is Jen and I've been with her for just over 4 years. She's been my rock, and is constantly changing high-schooler's lives as an English teacher (writing teacher, to be specific) back in Wisconsin.


That's bit of a scattered bio, but you get the picture. I'd be happy to tell you more over a good ale at some hipster microbrewery, but I'd much prefer a luke-warm can-of-whatever after climbing a few pitches.
